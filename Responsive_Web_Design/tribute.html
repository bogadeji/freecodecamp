laying down a life, saving a nation

Dr Stella Ameyo Adadevoh

Life and Times of Dr Adadevoh

  • 1956 - Born in Lagos, Nigeria
  • 1980 MBBS University of Lagos College of Medicine
  • 1981 - Housemanship Lagos University Teaching Hospital
  • 1982 – National Youth Service Corps, Eti-Osa Health Centre Lagos, Nigeria
  • 1983 - 1988 –Residency, Lagos University Teaching Hospital
  • 1986 – Married Afolabi Emmanuel Cardoso
  • 1988 – Son, Bankole Cardoso is born
  • 1989 – 1991 – Consultant, Lagos University Teaching Hospital
  • 1993 – Completion, fellowship in Endocrinology, Hammersmith Hospital of the Imperial College London, UK
  • Joined First Consultants Medical Centre, Obalende Lagos. Worked there until death in 2014
  • 2012 – First doctor to diagnose and alert the Ministry of Health on the spread of H1N1 (swine flu) into Nigeria
  • 2014 – Correctly diagnosed Nigeria’s index patient of Ebola Virus Disease ensuring His detainment with Hospital facilities, in the face of relentless pressure to release Him. She came into contact with EVD in this manner, leading eventually to her death. This action of hers however ensured strategic containment of the virus in a country unprepared and ill-equipped to face a full-blown exposure to the virus.
  • 2014 - dies at the age of 57
